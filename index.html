Enter live vehicle telemetry and get an instant prediction of whether the car is drifting — powered by a Random Forest model trained on sensor and driving-condition data.

ℹ️ About this project

What is drift?

Drifting happens when a car's rear wheels lose traction — often during sharp turns, high speeds, or sudden steering changes. Detecting it early matters for vehicle safety and for driver-assistance systems that need to react before control is lost.

What the model looks at

22 signals from the car's sensors: speed and acceleration, steering and rotation (yaw), all four wheel speeds, throttle/brake/gear, road conditions (curvature, friction, slope), and a few derived traction/slip metrics.

How it was trained

  • Split 60% train / 20% validation / 20% test
  • SMOTE to balance the rarer "drifting" class
  • Random Forest tuned via cross-validated random search
  • Decision threshold tuned on validation data, then scored once on held-out test data for an honest result
